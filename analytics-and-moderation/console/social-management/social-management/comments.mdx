## Accessing Comments

There are two ways to access comments in the Console:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="From a Post">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open Posts & Comments">Go to Admin Console → **Posts and comments**.</Step>
      <Step title="Find Post">Use the filter options (feed type, creator, post status) or search to locate the target post.</Step>
      <Step title="Open Comment Panel">Click the **comment count** (💬 icon with number) on any post card. A panel slides open on the right showing the full comment thread for that post.</Step>
      <Step title="Review">Identify flagged or high‑activity threads first. Each comment shows its AI Moderation status.</Step>
    </Steps>

    <Frame caption="Posts page with comment count button visible on each post card">
      <img src="/images/analytics-and-moderation/console/posts-page-overview.png" alt="Posts page overview" />
    </Frame>

    <Frame caption="Comment panel opens to the right, showing the parent post and all associated comments">
      <img src="/images/analytics-and-moderation/console/comments-drawer-open.png" alt="Comment panel" />
    </Frame>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="From Comments & Replies Tab">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open Posts & Comments">Go to Admin Console → **Posts and comments**.</Step>
      <Step title="Switch Tab">Select the **Comments & Replies** tab at the top of the page. This shows all comments and replies across all posts in a single list view.</Step>
      <Step title="Browse or Filter">Use the available filters to narrow down comments by status or other criteria.</Step>
    </Steps>

    <Frame caption="Posts page - comment and replies tab, showing a list of all comments and replies across posts with filter options">

    </Frame>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Comment Thread Structure

Comments support **multi-level threading** — replies can be nested multiple levels deep, creating branching conversations within a single post.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Displayed Data">
    Each comment or reply shows:
    - **Author photo & name** — click to view the user's history
    - **Timestamp** — date and time the comment was posted
    - **Comment text** — including **@mentions** (e.g., `@username`) that link to the mentioned user
    - **AI Moderation status** — shows "AI Mod: Passed" in green, or the flagged category (e.g., "Harassment or bullying")
    - **Engagement counts** — number of reactions and replies; click to expand replies
    - **Flag button** — quickly flag a comment for review
    - **More options menu** (⋮) — additional actions like edit, delete, and copy ID (see [Moderation Actions](#moderation-actions))
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Multi-Level Threading">
    Comments are organized in a **nested tree structure**:
    - **Level 1**: Top-level comments on a post
    - **Level 2**: Replies to top-level comments
    - **Level 3+**: Replies to replies, allowing deep conversational threads

    Each level is shown with:
    - **Indentation** and a reply arrow (↩) to show which comment is being replied to
    - **"X replies" link** — click to expand or collapse replies underneath
    - **"Hide replies"** — collapse an expanded reply thread to keep the view tidy

    <Frame caption="Multi-level comment thread showing three levels of nesting with @mentions and AI moderation status">
      <img src="/images/analytics-and-moderation/console/comments-deeper-level.png" alt="Multi-level comments expanded" />
    </Frame>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="@Mentions">
    Users can mention other users in comments and replies using the `@username` syntax. Mentions are:
    - Displayed as **clickable links** that navigate to the mentioned user's profile/history
    - Visually distinguished from regular text
    - Supported at all comment nesting levels
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Engagement Signals">
    Use reaction & reply counts to surface:
    - Emerging discussions needing moderation
    - Popular threads for community amplification
    - Potential hotspots for policy enforcement
    - Deep reply chains that may indicate heated discussions
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="AI Content Moderation">
    Every comment and reply is automatically checked by the AI moderation system:
    - **Passed** — a green "AI Mod: Passed" label appears on the comment
    - **Flagged** — the specific violation category is shown (e.g., "Harassment or bullying")
    - This status is visible on every comment at any nesting level
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Flag Handling">
    - Flagged comments are highlighted in the thread
    - Each comment has a **flag button** for quick flagging
    - Prioritize review based on severity & volume
    - Clearing a flag removes it from escalation metrics
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Moderation Actions

Each comment and reply has a **more options menu** (⋮) with actions tailored to the item. The wording adjusts depending on whether it is a top-level comment or a nested reply.

<Frame caption="Comment action menu showing Flag, Edit, Copy ID, and Delete options">
  <img src="/images/analytics-and-moderation/console/comments-comment-action-menu.png" alt="Comment action menu" />
</Frame>

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Flag">
    **Flag comment** / **Flag reply** — mark content for review. Also available via the flag button next to the more options menu.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Edit">
    **Edit comment** / **Edit reply** — modify the text content of the selected comment or reply.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Copy ID">
    **Copy comment ID** / **Copy reply ID** — copy the unique identifier for reference or internal tracking.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Delete">
    **Delete this comment** / **Delete this reply** — permanently remove the content. This action is immediate and cannot be undone. Shown in red as a warning.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Replying & Engagement

The comment panel includes a **comment bar** at the bottom where you can select your identity, type your message, and send.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Identity">Click your **profile picture** at the bottom-left of the panel. A "Select your profile" menu appears with two sections: **MYSELF** (your admin account) and **BRAND** (your official brand account).</Step>
  <Step title="Compose Message">Type in the "Start commenting here..." field. You can use **@mentions** to tag other users. The Send button becomes active once you start typing.</Step>
  <Step title="Send">Click **Send** to publish. Your comment or reply appears immediately in the right place within the thread.</Step>
</Steps>

<Frame caption="Identity selector showing MYSELF (admin) and BRAND profile options">
  <img src="/images/analytics-and-moderation/console/comments-identity-selector.png" alt="Identity selector dropdown" />
</Frame>

### Identity Usage

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Myself (Admin User)">
    Found under the **MYSELF** section. Shows your admin name and photo. Use for routine clarifications, community encouragement, and light moderation nudges.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Brand (Official)">
    Found under the **BRAND** section, marked with a verified badge. Use for formal statements, policy clarifications, resolution confirmations, and official announcements.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Switching Guidance">
    You can switch your identity at any time from the comment bar. Avoid switching identities mid-thread unless you need to escalate the tone (e.g., from a casual response to a formal resolution). Always check which identity is selected before sending.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Info>Brand profile requires prior configuration in Settings → Brand Settings. If no brand is configured, only the MYSELF option will appear.</Info>

## Quality & Tone Guidelines

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Clarity">Use short sentences. Lead with resolution or key message. Avoid jargon.</Tab>
  <Tab title="Neutrality">Stay impartial especially in disputes. Do not take sides publicly prematurely.</Tab>
  <Tab title="De-escalation">Acknowledge concerns, restate guidelines, offer constructive next steps.</Tab>
  <Tab title="Brand Consistency">Match approved voice: professional, concise, empathetic when needed.</Tab>
  <Tab title="Audit Readiness">For sensitive removals, maintain internal notes (external system) referencing rationale.</Tab>
</Tabs>

## Monitoring & Metrics

Track:
- Comment volume per high-traffic posts
- Reaction-to-reply ratio (engagement depth)
- Flag rate (flags per 100 comments)
- Response latency (time to first moderator/admin reply)
- Recurring offenders (repeat policy friction)

<Info>Use metrics trends to refine staffing schedules and escalation thresholds.</Info>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Operational Cadence">
    - Review high-activity posts at scheduled intervals.
    - Prioritize flagged + high-engagement threads.
    - Batch moderation actions to reduce context switching.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Consistency">
    - Apply deletion criteria uniformly.
    - Use saved internal templates for common responses (if available).
    - Avoid over-moderating constructive critique.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Community Health">
    - Encourage positive contributors with timely acknowledgment.
    - Address misinformation early with factual, sourced replies.
    - Track topics generating recurring friction for potential policy updates.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Risk Management">
    - Escalate threats, self-harm, or legal incidents per protocol.
    - Archive (externally) severe violation context before deletion.
    - Document patterns leading to account-level enforcement.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Likely Cause | Resolution |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| Comment not appearing | Network delay / stale view | Refresh thread; confirm submission |
| Cannot switch to Brand | Brand not configured / permission | Configure Brand or adjust role |
| Reply posted under wrong identity | Identity not re-selected | Verify identity before sending |
| Flag won’t clear | Insufficient permission / state change | Confirm role & refresh thread |
| High duplicate spam | Automated/bot activity | Apply broader anti-spam measures / escalate |
| Reactions not updating | Cached metrics | Refresh or wait for sync cycle |
| Deleted comment inquiries | User confusion | Provide neutral explanation referencing guidelines |
